Different Ways You Can Help End Pet Homelessness

Millions of pets end up in shelters every year across the world. Being a pet-friendly community starts with working to end pet homelessness. Through our special program, we work to help cities tackle pet homelessness and increase adoption.

Here are seven ways you can help in your community.

1. Know the causes of pet homelessness.

Most pets who are homeless got there through no fault of their own. From financial strain, to owner illness to housing issues, life changes for owners affect many pets. Initiatives that help address these causes, like pet-friendly housing and support for pet owners facing financial challenges, can help.

2. Spay and neuter pets.

Spaying and neutering pets can help reduce unplanned litters that could end up homeless. It’s not right for every pet, so you’ll want to talk with your veterinarian. But for many pets it’s a helpful step that can even offer health benefits. You can see more about the potential benefits of spay/neuter from your local pet clinic.

3. Adopt.

If you’re looking to add a pet to your family, consider adopting from a shelter or rescue. Not only do you give a pet a second chance, you can find a pet that’s a great match to your family. Chose the age, personality and activity level you want since there are so many homeless pets to choose from. You can also adopt a pet through our platform here.

4. Be a volunteer.

Shelters and rescues need help to support the many homeless pets they assist every year. As a volunteer, you can help pets get exercise, be socialized and even spread the word to encourage their adoption. You can even make volunteering a family project.

5. Donate to help homeless pets.

Organizations across the world work tirelessly to help, but with millions of pets becoming homeless each year, they need your support. Donate to your local shelter or to organizations that offer grants to many shelters and rescues that help pets get adopted.
